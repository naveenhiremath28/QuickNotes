

# 1️⃣ What is a **Blockchain**?

A **blockchain** is like a public digital ledger (record book).

It:

- Stores transactions
    
- Is shared across thousands of computers
    
- Cannot be easily changed or hacked
    
- Doesn’t need a central authority (like a bank)
    

Think of it like:

> 📒 A Google Sheet that everyone can see, nobody owns, and no one can secretly edit.


**Ethereum** is a blockchain.

It allows:

- Sending digital money (ETH)
    
- Running smart contracts (programs on blockchain)
    
- Building decentralized apps (dApps)
    

ETH is the cryptocurrency used on Ethereum.

**Solana** is another blockchain.

It is known for:

- Very fast transactions
    
- Very low fees
    
- High scalability
    

Its cryptocurrency is called SOL.

---

# 2️⃣ What is a Wallet?

A **crypto wallet** is NOT where your crypto is stored.

Important:

> Your crypto is stored on the blockchain.  
> Your wallet stores the **keys** that give access to it.

Think of it like:

|Real World|Crypto World|
|---|---|
|Bank account|Blockchain|
|ATM card|Wallet|
|PIN|Private key|

---

## Wallet contains:

- 🔑 **Public Key (Address)** → Like your account number (you can share it)
    
- 🔒 **Private Key** → Like your password (never share this)
    

If someone gets your private key → they control your crypto.

---

# 3️⃣ Types of Wallets

### 🔹 Software Wallets (Hot Wallets)

Examples:

- **MetaMask**
    
- **Phantom**
    

They:

- Run as browser extensions or apps
    
- Are connected to internet
    
- Easy to use
    

Good for daily usage.

---

### 🔹 Hardware Wallets (Cold Wallets)

Examples:

- **Ledger Nano X**
    
- **Trezor Model T**
    

They:

- Are physical devices
    
- Store private keys offline
    
- Very secure
    

Best for long-term storage.

---

# 4️⃣ How Everything Connects

Here’s the simple flow:

1. Blockchain (Ethereum / Solana)  
    → Stores transactions and balances
    
2. Wallet  
    → Holds your private key  
    → Signs transactions
    
3. When you send crypto:
    
    - Wallet signs the transaction
        
    - Blockchain verifies it
        
    - Network updates the ledger
